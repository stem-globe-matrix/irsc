IRSC Scientific Standards Repository

The IRSC Standards Repository contains structured scientific frameworks, ethical compliance procedures, publication regulations, validation methodologies, and institutional operational guidelines intended to support responsible scientific development, technical transparency, reproducibility, and research integrity.

Published Standards & Frameworks

All IRSC standards are independently developed procedural frameworks intended to support scientific responsibility, ethical compliance, transparent innovation, reproducibility systems, and institutional research integrity mechanisms.

IRSC-AI-101

Artificial Intelligence Ethics Framework

Standards governing ethical AI development and responsible computational deployment.

  • Mandatory disclosure of AI-assisted methodologies.
  • Algorithmic transparency should be maintained where feasible.
  • Bias assessment documentation shall accompany datasets.
  • Human oversight systems must exist for critical operations.
IRSC-RP-201

Research Publication Standards

Frameworks supporting scientific documentation and publication integrity.

  • All citations must follow recognized academic structures.
  • Fabricated references are prohibited.
  • Methodology sections should provide reproducible clarity.
  • Conflict of interest disclosures are mandatory.
IRSC-DV-301

Data Verification Protocols

Procedures supporting reproducibility and dataset integrity verification.

  • Datasets shall maintain source traceability.
  • Experimental data modification logs should be retained.
  • Verification samples may be requested during review.
  • Statistical inconsistencies require procedural disclosure.
IRSC-ES-401

Ethics & Scientific Integrity Standards

Ethical compliance systems supporting research authenticity and transparency.

  • Plagiarism exceeding acceptable thresholds is prohibited.
  • Manipulation of findings constitutes procedural violation.
  • Ethics declarations shall accompany human-related studies.
  • Undisclosed sponsorship influence is restricted.
IRSC-CS-501

Cybersecurity Research Standards

Frameworks governing responsible cybersecurity research practices.

  • Unauthorized exploitation testing is prohibited.
  • Research must avoid infrastructure disruption.
  • Vulnerability disclosures should follow responsible timelines.
  • Research data confidentiality must be preserved.
IRSC-ST-601

STEM Innovation Validation Framework

Validation methodologies for emerging scientific and engineering innovations.

  • Prototype claims require demonstrable evidence.
  • Performance metrics should be measurable and documented.
  • Safety assessments shall accompany engineering systems.
  • Testing conditions must be transparently disclosed.
IRSC-OS-701

Open Science & Accessibility Standards

Frameworks supporting transparency and collaborative scientific accessibility.

  • Research summaries should remain publicly understandable.
  • Open-access publication is encouraged where feasible.
  • Data sharing limitations must be disclosed.
  • Accessibility accommodations should be considered.
IRSC-BT-801

Biotechnology Research Procedures

Standards addressing responsible biotechnology experimentation.

  • Biohazard containment procedures must be followed.
  • Human genetic data confidentiality is mandatory.
  • Unauthorized biological release testing is prohibited.
  • Ethical approval documentation shall be retained.
IRSC-ED-901

Educational Research Integrity Framework

Standards governing academic and educational research systems.

  • Student research attribution must remain accurate.
  • Educational experiments require informed consent.
  • Evaluation metrics shall remain transparent.
  • Academic falsification is prohibited.
IRSC-QA-1001

Quality Assurance & Review Systems

Institutional quality assurance methodologies for research evaluation.

  • Independent review systems are recommended.
  • Documentation revisions should maintain version history.
  • Review timelines should remain procedurally fair.
  • Appeal mechanisms may exist for disputed reviews.
IRSC-PV-1101

Prototype Validation & Conditional Certification Framework

Procedural standards governing conditional research certification for projects, theoretical systems, engineering concepts, computational architectures, scientific models, and emerging technological proposals where immediate demonstrative prototype generation may not be reasonably achievable due to technical, infrastructural, computational, laboratory, engineering, or developmental complexity.

  • Research submissions demonstrating sufficient scientific reasoning, structured methodology, technical feasibility, innovation potential, and documentation integrity may qualify for Conditional Validation Certification status.
  • Conditional certification may be issued where prototype generation requires extended engineering cycles, specialized infrastructure, computational systems, laboratory environments, hardware fabrication processes, or multi-stage implementation procedures.
  • Projects granted Conditional Validation Certification shall submit a functional demonstrative prototype, technical implementation evidence, simulation framework, proof-of-concept system, experimental validation, or equivalent demonstrative material within a maximum duration of sixty (60) calendar days from certification issuance unless formally extended through administrative review procedures.
  • Failure to provide required validation materials within the designated review duration may result in certificate suspension, inactive certification status, procedural withdrawal, revocation of conditional validation, or mandatory re-evaluation under applicable IRSC verification frameworks.
  • Conditional certification status shall not constitute final technical endorsement, scientific proof, institutional guarantee, regulatory approval, commercial authorization, deployment certification, or operational clearance unless supported through completed validation review procedures.

Institutional Clarification & Administrative Status

The International Research Standards Council (IRSC) is an independently operated and privately held standards-oriented committee established for the purpose of encouraging scientific integrity, structured validation systems, ethical research conduct, and responsible publication methodologies.

IRSC does not represent governmental authority, statutory accreditation, or legally binding regulatory enforcement unless explicitly recognized by applicable jurisdictions or institutions.

All IRSC frameworks, standards, procedural advisories, institutional methodologies, and scientific review systems are intended to support responsible research ecosystems, voluntary compliance structures, transparent innovation systems, and scientific accountability practices.
